# C&A Brasil E-Commerce Research — 2018–2020

**Researched:** 2026-02-18

## Brazilian Market Context (2018–2019)

- **Parcelamento (instalment plans):** 70%+ of Brazilian e-commerce purchases made in instalments
- **Credit card penetration:** below 50% of the adult population
- **Boleto bancário:** used for ~30–40% of Brazilian e-commerce transactions in 2018
- **Mobile commerce share:** ~37–43% of all e-commerce transactions in 2018–2019 (grew from 35.3% to 43.1% June 2018 → June 2019)
- **Total e-commerce market:** R$53.2B in 2018 (+12% YoY), R$61.9B in 2019
- **Fashion mobile:** only ~20% of fashion online sales in Brazil via mobile in 2019 (vs. Japan 62%, UK 55%, USA 43%) — significant runway

## C&A Brasil Specific Data

- **2019 IPO:** Listed on B3 Novo Mercado (CEAB3), raised R$1.8B, priced at R$16.50/share
- **Total net revenue 2019:** R$5.2B (+2.8% vs 2018)
- **Net profit 2019:** R$214.2M (+23.4%)
- **220 stores** at IPO filing
- Ship-from-Store launched 2018
- No specific e-commerce revenue % or digital GMV disclosed in publicly accessible IPO summaries

## Post-Rodrigo (context only, not attributable)

- E-commerce revenue grew **297%** in 2020 vs 2019 (pandemic-driven)
- App grew from 400K MAU (Q1 2020) to 3M MAU (Q2 2020)
- Became most downloaded fashion app in Brazil in 2020
- R$1.07B digital net revenue milestone reached in 2023
